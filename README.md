## 📋 Core Presentation Agenda & Structure

### 1. Introduction & Theoretical Framework
*   **The Problem:** Addressing shifting technological demands in higher education and analyzing student access channels.
*   **Pedagogical Baseline:** Grounding technology integration within constructivist learning models, progressivism, and cognitive "MindTools."
*   **Focus Area:** Assessing mobile technology trends and shifting physical learning spaces into connected communities.

### 2. Literature Review Summary
*   **Audio Pedagogy:** Synthesizing literature surrounding the instructional effectiveness of rich media and web services.
*   **The Digital Divide:** Addressing socioeconomic impacts on computer access, internet utilization, and community college persistence.

### 3. Methodology & Research Design
*   **Approach:** Quantitative synthesis and correlation models evaluating educational technology deployment.
*   **Metrics:** Analysis of student performance metrics, utilizing statistical indicators such as Z-scores to identify performance variances.
*   **Target Sample:** Evaluating mobile tool efficacy across undergraduate cohorts and community learning college systems.

### 4. Case Study Analysis: Classroom Podcasting
*   **Core Study:** A dedicated case study examining classroom podcasting infrastructure, platform deployment (e.g., Spreaker), and direct correlation to student learning outcomes.
*   **Key Findings:** Strategic timing of software deployment and technical support loops for both faculty and students dramatically impact transfer of learning.

### 5. Strategic Recommendations & Future Horizons
*   **Organizational Management:** Frameworks for administrative restructuring using strategic choice theory and structural organizational frames to manage educational technology budgets.
*   **Future Trends:** Predictions on enterprise mobility, web services evolution, and automated technical training frameworks.

### 7. NASA/CREST Beacon Data Processing System (BDPS)

[![NASA Ames Honor Award](https://img.shields.io/badge/Award-NASA%20Ames%20Honor%20Award-blue.svg)](#acknowledgments)

*   The **Beacon Data Processing System (BDPS)** is a multi-tier telemetry ingestion and decoding platform developed by a visionary student engineering team at **Ohlone College** in collaboration with **NASA/CREST**. The system is specifically engineered to receive, decode, and catalog public beacon telemetry data submitted by ground stations and amateur radio operators tracking the **PharmaSat spacecraft**. 

*   The platform exposes open, interoperable APIs allowing external developers, schools, and NASA to programmatically leverage the system's core business logic. This project was recognized with the prestigious **NASA Ames Honor Award** for its contribution to collaborative aerospace data collection and open systems architecture.
